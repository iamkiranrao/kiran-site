Instagram

The feed that forgot what it was for - and how to bring it back

How I'd've Built It / Meta / Instagram
Product at a Glance
Visual content platform and social commerce engine. Instagram started as a place to share filtered photos in order - you'd see posts from people you followed. Now it's Meta's main way to keep under-35s engaged, mostly through algorithmically chosen videos and content instead of what they actually followed.

Section 01
Discovery
Persona

Maya R.
29, marketing manager in Austin, daily IG user since 2018
Follows 400+ accounts - college friends, food blogs, a couple of travel photographers she found in 2019. Sees maybe 20 of them in her feed. Hasn't posted since her friend's birthday in October. Opens the app 8-10 times a day, usually without realizing she's doing it.

After reading through roughly 200 App Store reviews and an academic study analyzing 1,100+ Reddit comments about the Instagram algorithm, the loudest voice wasn't creators complaining about reach. It was regular users describing a relationship with the app that felt out of their control. Maya represents that - the person who doesn't complain on Twitter but gradually disengages.

I initially looked at content moderation as the primary pain point, but the review data pointed somewhere else. The word "algorithm" shows up in about 1 in 4 one-star reviews. That's where I focused.

Section 02
Keep / Kill / Build

I spent two hours reading App Store reviews for this section. The patterns were clear but the priority wasn't obvious - so I want to be transparent about what I chose to focus on and what I dropped. I initially considered content moderation and account suspensions as primary issues, but the review data pointed much more strongly to the algorithm and scroll fatigue. So I followed the data.

Keep
Following & Favorites feeds
Instagram added these in March 2022. They work - chronological, only accounts you chose. The problem isn't the feature, it's that it resets every session and can't be set as your default. That's a choice Instagram made, and it's the choice this teardown is challenging.
Stories format
Stories have a beginning and an end. You run out of them. This is the one format on Instagram that respects the user's time.
Kill
Algorithmic feed as the default
This is where the problem starts. Users follow 400 accounts and see 20. Academic research on 1,100+ Reddit comments describes the algorithm as "anxiety-inducing, confusing, and violating." The Following feed exists but you have to manually switch to it every single session.
Explore with no exit ramp
Infinite scroll with zero stopping points. No "you're caught up," no session summary, no friction. This is the engine behind scroll fatigue.
Take a break buried and off by default
Five taps deep: Profile > Menu > Your Activity > Time > Set Reminder. Off by default. When your screen time tool requires a tutorial to find, it's not a feature - it's a checkbox.
Build
Flip the default
Following feed on open, algorithmic as opt-in. That's the core proposal. Everything else follows from this.
Session digest
After 15-20 minutes, a summary card: "Here's what you saw. Want to keep going?" Not a block - a mirror. Even a 15% exit rate here changes the dynamic from passive consumption to active choice.
Daily briefing
I almost didn't include this one. A finite, scrollable card stack of top posts from your Following list - like Stories but for posts. It ends. No major app does this yet, which is either a gap in the market or a sign that it doesn't work. I think it's a gap.
Section 03
The Redesign

Two wireframes. The first is the core bet - flip the feed default. The second is the novel idea - give users a reason to stop scrolling that doesn't require willpower.

Proposal 1 - Following-First Home Screen
Wireframe
Flip the default. That's it. Users land on Following (chronological, accounts they chose). The algorithmic "For You" feed is one tap away but not forced. Instagram shipped Following and Favorites tabs in March 2022, but buried them behind a dropdown that most users never found - and the tabs reset on every app launch. So a real, permanent toggle that remembers your choice? That's not a small change.
Proposal 2 - Session Digest
Wireframe
You've been scrolling for 20 minutes. Here's what happened. Want to keep going? This doesn't block anything - you can dismiss it and continue. But it breaks the autopilot. Because choosing to keep going is different from never being asked. BeReal's entire value proposition is built on this insight: users want intentionality, not just content.
Section 04
The Business Case
Executive Summary

This is a bet on retention over session time. Research shows algorithmic feeds drive significantly more daily time than chronological alternatives - and Meta's own internal research found users switch back to algorithmic within days when given the choice. So yes, there's real risk here. The argument is that session time drops but daily opens and long-term retention improve, because users who feel in control come back more often.

Hold flat or improve. The real risk is that algorithmic-feed users see a dip - but those users chose the algorithm, so the content is working for them.

The real problem is ads. Instagram's ad model is built on session time. Less time means fewer ad impressions. Meta doesn't publish session-level revenue breakdowns - their Q3 2024 earnings showed $39.9 billion in ad revenue, but there's no public way to model how feed changes would affect that number.

But here's my read: users who opt into Following mode are already disengaging from algorithmic content. you're not really losing ad revenue from engaged users - you're losing impressions from people who weren't paying attention. And the For You feed becomes a stronger ad environment when users choose it intentionally - intent-based browsing converts better than passive scrolling. This is the same insight that makes Google Search ads more valuable than display ads.

Assumptions & Limitations

I don't work at Instagram. I've never seen their internal engagement dashboards or experimentation roadmap. Everything here is built from public data - app store reviews (roughly 200 reviewed), the ACSI 2024 survey (76/100, trailing TikTok at 77), academic research (ACM study analyzing 1,100+ Reddit comments), and published platform statistics. If I had access to Meta's internal session data, the business case section would look different. The personas are composites based on review patterns, not real individuals. The KPI numbers are educated guesses - ranges, not predictions.
