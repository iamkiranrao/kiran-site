Fargo AI Assistant - Consumer Scale
Scaled the bank's AI assistant from 4.1M to 27.5M active users
27.5M users
Screenshot of the Fargo AI assistant in the Wells Fargo mobile app
AI Scale Mobile
The Problem
Fargo, the bank's AI assistant, had been live for two years but sat at 4.1M users - a fraction of the mobile app base. Support call volume was rising, and the assistant wasn't absorbing the load it was designed to handle. Customers either didn't know it existed or tried it once and didn't come back.
The Bet
We believed the problem wasn't the AI itself - it was discovery and first-use experience. If we surfaced Fargo contextually (inside transactions, alerts, and search) rather than burying it in a menu, and invested in response quality for the top 50 intents, adoption would follow.
What Shipped
Contextual entry points across the mobile app (transaction disputes, balance inquiries, bill pay). Rebuilt the top 50 conversation flows with improved NLU. Added proactive nudges based on user behavior patterns. Integrated Fargo responses into push notification workflows.
The Outcome
Scaled from 4.1M to 27.5M active users in under 18 months. Support volume dropped 17%, saving an estimated $12M annually in call center costs. Fargo became the primary self-service channel for balance and transaction inquiries.

Mobile Engagement Growth
The Problem
Push notifications were treated as an afterthought - generic, poorly timed, and high volume. Customers were disabling notifications entirely, which meant they missed fraud alerts, payment confirmations, and time-sensitive actions. Opt-out rates were climbing.
The Bet
Fewer, smarter notifications would outperform the blast approach. If we made every push actionable and contextually relevant - and gave users granular control over what they received - they'd keep notifications on and engage with them.

The Outcome

Biometrics & Cardless ATM

60% adoption
Security 0 to 1
The Problem
Password-based authentication was causing friction and security gaps. Customers were locking themselves out, and card skimming at ATMs was a growing fraud vector.
The Bet
Biometric authentication (fingerprint and face) combined with NFC cardless ATM would simultaneously improve security and reduce friction - two goals that usually compete.
What Shipped
Touch ID and Face ID integration for mobile login and transaction approval. NFC-based cardless ATM withdrawal across 13,000 ATMs. One-time-code fallback for non-biometric devices.
The Outcome
