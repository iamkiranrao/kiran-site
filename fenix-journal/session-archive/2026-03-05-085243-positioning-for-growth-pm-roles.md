Growth PM and Head of Growth roles are genuinely well-suited to your background — here's a clear picture of what companies want and how you map to it.

**What these roles actually own**

Rather than owning a specific product, a growth PM focuses on improving a specific business metric or commercial goal. Their purview covers the entire funnel — from new user acquisition all the way through customer retention and expansion, using short-term experiments to incrementally improve efficiencies throughout.

PMs in these roles are no longer simply tasked with delivering features — they're accountable for driving revenue and product success through growth metrics that directly impact the P&L.

**The core skill set companies screen for**

The ideal growth PM is laser-focused on numbers, treats failure as valuable insight, and must bridge engineers, designers, analysts, and marketers to get buy-in for growth experiments. Beyond that:

- **Experimentation mindset** — A/B testing, funnel optimization, high-velocity experiments
- **Data fluency** — cohort analysis, LTV, activation rates, retention metrics
- **Full-funnel ownership** — acquisition through monetization and churn
- **Cross-functional diplomacy** — particularly crucial when working with traditional PMs who may be territorial about their specific products or roadmaps

**How your background maps**

You're actually in a strong position here — arguably stronger than most candidates who come from pure startup backgrounds. Here's the translation:

| Your Experience | Growth PM Signal |
|---|---|
| Fargo AI: 4.1M → 27.5M users | You've *lived* a growth curve at scale |
| Mobile banking: 18M → 32M users | Proven acquisition + retention ownership |
| Regulated environment | Forces rigorous experimentation frameworks — a differentiator |
| Cross-functional at Wells Fargo | Exactly the diplomacy growth PMs need |

**The gaps to address in positioning**

Two things companies may wonder about:

1. **"Did you run experiments or just observe growth?"** — You'll want to lead with specific A/B tests, funnel experiments, or activation improvements you personally drove, not just the headline numbers.

2. **"Do you know PLG/SaaS growth loops?"** — The growth PM role is most immediately valuable within organizations that have embraced a product-led growth strategy — free trial or freemium models driving acquisition with low-touch sales. Banking doesn't use freemium, so you'll want to show you understand PLG mechanics conceptually and can apply them.

**How to position yourself**

- **Lead with the Fargo story as a growth narrative**, not a features-delivered story. Frame it around the growth loop: what drove initial activation, what caused drop-off, what experiments moved the needle.
- **Reframe "regulated environment" as an asset** — it means you built rigorous measurement and change management discipline that scrappy growth teams often lack.
- **Add PLG/growth vocabulary** to your resume and LinkedIn — terms like activation rate, D30 retention, referral loops, funnel conversion, paywall optimization signal fluency in the language.
- For the roles at companies like **Intuit, Airbnb, or Uber** on your target list, growth PM is a natural home — they have massive consumer funnels where your banking-at-scale experience translates directly.
