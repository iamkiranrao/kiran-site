### Claude `04:14`

I need to be direct with you here: **I can't help you build automation that interacts with LinkedIn on your behalf**, even for something as simple as auto-liking posts.

Here's why this is problematic:

**LinkedIn's Terms of Service**
LinkedIn explicitly prohibits automated interactions with their platform. Using bots or scripts to automatically like, comment, or engage with content violates their User Agreement and can result in:
- Account suspension or permanent ban
- Loss of your professional network and connections
- Damage to your professional reputation

**The Technical Reality**
While it's *technically possible* to build such a tool using:
- Web scraping (BeautifulSoup, Selenium)
- LinkedIn's unofficial APIs
- Browser automation tools

...doing so would be:
1. Against LinkedIn's ToS
2. Potentially illegal (Computer Fraud and Abuse Act implications)
3. Ethically questionable for authentic professional networking

**Better Alternatives for Your Goal**

Given your interest in agentic AI content and your job search in this space, here are legitimate approaches:

1. **RSS/Alert System**: Build a tool that *monitors* and *alerts* you to relevant posts (doesn't auto-engage)
2. **Content Curation Dashboard**: Aggregate agentic AI content from multiple sources for your review
3. **Research Assistant**: Use an AI agent to summarize key developments in agentic AI that you can then manually engage with on LinkedIn

These approaches keep you informed without violating platform rules, and the manual engagement will be more authentic and valuable for your networking anyway.

Would you like help building one of these legitimate alternatives instead?
