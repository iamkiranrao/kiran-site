**1. Conversational AI / Chatbot Experience**
This is the #1 thing they want. Be ready to talk about any experience building or managing chatbot, virtual assistant, or conversational AI products. If you have specific examples, prepare a concise story: the problem, your approach, the outcome (with metrics).

**2. Scale & Consumer-Facing**
GEICO serves millions. Highlight experience with high-traffic consumer products, especially mobile apps and web platforms. Mention MAU/DAU numbers or transaction volumes if you can.

**3. Digital Transformation / Ambiguity**
They describe this as a "startup-like" environment within a large company. They want a builder who can operate without a playbook. Have examples of times you built something from scratch or led in ambiguous situations.

**4. Cross-Functional Leadership**
They want someone who partners deeply with engineering, design, ops, and business stakeholders. Prepare stories about influencing without authority and driving alignment across teams.

**5. Data-Driven Decision Making**
Be ready to discuss KPIs you've owned — containment rate, deflection rate, CSAT, NPS, task completion rate are all relevant chatbot metrics. Talk about how data shaped your roadmap.

You're right — I missed the "Questions You Should Ask the Recruiter" and "Quick Research Talking Points" sections. Here's how to deliver those:

---

**Questions to Ask the Recruiter:**

**"Where is the chatbot product today?"**
> Ask this early — it shapes everything. You want to know: "Is this a build-from-scratch situation, or is there an existing chatbot I'd be iterating on? What platform or tech stack is it running on today?" This also signals you're already thinking about the work.

**"What's the team structure?"**
> "Can you give me a sense of the team I'd be working with — how many engineers, designers, data scientists? Is the team already stood up or still being built?" This tells you whether you're hiring and building vs. inheriting a team.

**"Who does this role report to?"**
> "Who would I be reporting to — is it a VP of Product, a GM, or someone else?" This helps you understand org structure and how much executive access you'll have.

**"What does success look like in the first 6-12 months?"**
> "If you hired someone great for this role, what would they have accomplished in their first year?" This shows you're outcome-oriented and also helps you gauge expectations.

**"How far along is the broader digital transformation?"**
> "GEICO talks a lot about its tech transformation — where would you say you are in that journey? Early stages, mid-flight?" This shows you've done your homework and are thinking beyond just your product.

---

**Quick Research Talking Points to Weave In:**

> "I know GEICO has historically been a direct-to-consumer leader — the no-agent model — so it makes sense that digital self-service and conversational AI would be a strategic priority."

> "I've also noticed competitors like Lemonade are AI-native, and Progressive and State Farm have been investing heavily in digital servicing. There's a real window for GEICO to leapfrog here given the customer base size."

> "Being part of Berkshire Hathaway is interesting too — there's financial stability to make long-term bets on technology, which isn't always the case at companies going through transformation."

These are best dropped naturally, not listed off. For example, when they ask "why GEICO," you can fold in the Berkshire Hathaway point or the competitive landscape observation.
