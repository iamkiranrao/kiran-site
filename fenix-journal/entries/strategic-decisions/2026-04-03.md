# Strategic Decisions

### Two-Tier Unlock Architecture: Anonymous + Connected
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Product Philosophy | Architecture

**Decision:** The persona unlock system will have two tiers — "Explore freely" (anonymous, immediate value) and "Connect to unlock" (identity-gated features). This replaces the PERSONA-PLAYBOOK's current model where all Track 2 unlocks are freely available after persona selection.

**Context:** The original PERSONA-PLAYBOOK treated all per-persona functional unlocks as ungated. Kiran envisioned a system where some features require the visitor to identify themselves, creating a natural conversion mechanism from anonymous persona to known person. The challenge: this could feel transactional, conflicting with SITE-WHY's relational principle.

**Reasoning:** The gate must be *functional*, not artificial. Connected unlocks are features that genuinely work better with identity context — a Fit Score needs a specific job description, "Bring Your Problem" needs company context, a pairing session needs to know who's pairing. Anonymous unlocks should be genuinely generous — the visitor should feel the anonymous tier was worth their time regardless. The emotional pull toward connecting comes from wanting more of this quality, not from feeling tricked.

**Impact:** Fundamentally changes the persona unlock component, the Fenix introduction flow, and the Connect module. Requires backend support for identity verification and a notification pipeline to Command Center. All six personas need redesigned unlock lists with anonymous/connected split.

**Open Questions:** The specific unlocks for each persona need per-persona deep dives. The personality layer (non-utility anonymous features) needs creative development per persona.

### Northstar Metric: Personas Become Persons
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** The unlock system's success metric is "how many personas become persons" — the rate at which anonymous visitors reveal their identity through the Connect flow.

**Context:** SITE-WHY.md defines the northstar as "how many people click Connect." Kiran sharpened this: the transformation from persona (anonymous archetype) to person (identified individual) is the core conversion event. This reframes Connect not as a UI action but as an identity transformation.

**Reasoning:** "Clicking Connect" is a button metric. "Personas becoming persons" is a relationship metric. It changes how you evaluate the unlock system: not "did they click?" but "did they trust us enough to reveal who they are?" This aligns with the relational principle — the conversion is about trust and value exchange, not lead capture.

**Impact:** This becomes the primary success metric for the entire unlock/Fenix/Connect system. Design decisions should be evaluated against it.

**Open Questions:** How to measure this technically (OAuth callback + form submissions → CC notification pipeline). What constitutes a "quality" conversion (is a fake name in the form a failure?).

### Dual Connect Mechanism: OAuth + Introduce Yourself
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Architecture | Product Philosophy

**Decision:** Offer two parallel paths for identity verification: LinkedIn/Google OAuth (instant, verified) and a simple "introduce yourself" form (name + company, warm, human). Fenix presents both options and the visitor chooses.

**Context:** Three approaches were evaluated: (A) OAuth only — low friction, high verification, but feels like SaaS onboarding; (B) LinkedIn connection request — most relational but highest friction with context switches; (C) Simple "introduce yourself" form — warm and human but lower verification. Kiran's reaction: combine A and C to respect both efficiency and warmth.

**Reasoning:** Different visitors have different comfort levels. A recruiter might prefer LinkedIn OAuth (they live there). A founder might prefer typing their name (less corporate). Offering both means the Connect moment adapts to the visitor's preference, not the site's preference. Fenix can frame it: "Sign in with LinkedIn for instant access, or just tell me who you are."

**Impact:** Backend needs to support two identity flows feeding into one notification pipeline. OAuth requires LinkedIn/Google developer app registration. The form path needs spam mitigation without CAPTCHAs (the persona selection itself is a mild filter — bots don't pick personas).

**Open Questions:** Whether Google OAuth adds meaningful value or dilutes the professional context that LinkedIn provides. Whether the "introduce yourself" form should include LinkedIn URL as an optional field (gives verification without the OAuth flow).

### Personality in the Anonymous Tier
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Product Philosophy | Brand/Identity

**Decision:** Every persona's anonymous unlock list must include at least one "personality feature" — something unexpected, delightful, and human that makes Kiran feel like a real person, not a portfolio.

**Context:** The question was whether anonymous unlocks should be purely utilitarian (resume, frameworks, case studies) or include personality-driven features. If all-utility, the site feels like every other portfolio with better packaging. If personality is present, it creates emotional residue — the thing that makes someone think "I want to know more about this person."

**Reasoning:** The anonymous tier is where personality belongs. The connected tier is where job-to-be-done belongs. The personality features create the emotional pull that makes someone *want* to connect. The utility features create professional credibility. Together, they make the connected unlock feel like the obvious next step, not a paywall.

**Impact:** Each of the six personas needs a creative personality feature designed during their deep dive. These features may require the most creative development and are the highest "Claude fingerprint" risk — meaning Kiran may need to source the ideas himself or through external creative tools.

**Open Questions:** Specific personality features for each persona. These need to feel distinctly Kiran — not generic "fun portfolio tricks."

### Fenix as Conversion Engine, Not Chatbot
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** Fenix's introduction in the unlock module is its first real moment with the visitor. It's not a support chatbot or a portfolio FAQ — it's a persona-aware concierge that guides visitors through unlock options and, when appropriate, through the Connect conversion.

**Context:** Fenix was previously positioned as a general-purpose AI assistant (ask me anything about Kiran). The unlock architecture reframes it: Fenix has specific pills that map 1:1 to unlock options, persona-specific introductions, and a conversational flow that naturally leads toward the Connect moment for connected features.

**Reasoning:** A general chatbot is a toy. A concierge with specific capabilities, persona awareness, and a conversion path is a product feature. Fenix explaining "this feature needs your context to work well — connect so I can give you something actually useful" is a value proposition, not a paywall pitch. The persona-aware framing means each visitor feels like Fenix understands their specific needs.

**Impact:** Fenix's system prompt needs persona-specific modes, not just greeting variations. The pill-based navigation means Fenix needs defined conversation flows for each unlock, both anonymous and connected. The Connect flow needs to be embeddable within Fenix's conversation.

**Open Questions:** How deep should Fenix's persona-specific behavior go? Greeting + pills, or fundamentally different conversation styles? How to handle a visitor who ignores the pills and asks a freeform question — does Fenix still steer toward unlocks?

### Per-Persona Deep Dive Process
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Architecture

**Decision:** The unlock strategy will be developed through individual deep dives, one persona at a time, across multiple sessions. An UNLOCK-STRATEGY.md doc will carry context between sessions.

**Context:** Kiran explicitly said "I want to take it slow. Don't want to rush this. Go persona by persona. Go deep and identify the right user options and end to end journeys." This was a response to Claude having already laid out a comprehensive per-persona table — premature, in Kiran's view.

**Reasoning:** The unlock system is the most consequential product decision for the site. Each persona has different needs, different emotional registers, and different conversion dynamics. Rushing through all six in one session would produce surface-level thinking. Going deep on each one — mapping the full journey from unlock list to Fenix conversation to Connect moment — produces a system that feels individually crafted, not templated.

**Impact:** Strategy work will span multiple sessions. UNLOCK-STRATEGY.md becomes a living document updated after each persona deep dive. No code is written until the strategy is complete for all six personas.

**Open Questions:** Which persona to start with. Session suggested Evaluator (highest stakes) but left the choice to Kiran.

### Resume as Lens System, Not Single Download
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Product Philosophy | Architecture

**Decision:** The Evaluator's utility unlock is a resume lens system — the same career presented through different focal points (AI PM, Growth PM, Product Strategy), each in traditional PDF format, plus an unconventional rendered page version that includes site work as portfolio evidence.

**Context:** The initial proposal was "resume download" as a simple anonymous unlock. Kiran immediately deepened it: "Trust is paramount here. This person is doing their job." He rejected a generic one-size-fits-all resume in favor of variants that foreground different aspects of his experience depending on the role type being recruited for. The unconventional version (a rendered page rather than PDF) serves as proof that Kiran builds product, not just talks about it.

**Reasoning:** Recruiters are evaluating dozens of candidates. A lens system respects their time by surfacing the most relevant experience for their specific opening. The traditional PDF format respects their workflow (ATS systems, forwarding to hiring managers). The unconventional version is for the recruiter who wants to go deeper — it's the site itself serving as evidence of product capability. Both formats serve trust: the PDF says "I respect your process," the rendered page says "and I can also show, not just tell."

**Impact:** Resume creation needs to happen across 3+ lens variants. The unconventional rendered page is a new build requirement (essentially a dedicated resume page that includes dynamic site elements). Email-to-self functionality suggested for the unconventional version.

**Open Questions:** How many lens variants are enough? Do they differ only in bullet emphasis or also in ordering and structure? Does the rendered version need its own URL or is it part of the unlock flow?

### Dual Fit Score: Mutual Evaluation as Connected Unlock
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Product Philosophy | Brand/Identity

**Decision:** The Evaluator's connected unlock is a Dual Fit Score — not just "does Kiran fit your role?" but also "does your role fit Kiran?" The recruiter pastes a job description, Fenix analyzes the bidirectional match, and both sides of the evaluation are surfaced.

**Context:** Claude presented 5 options for the connected unlock (A: simple fit score, B: Fenix diagnostic mode, C: role simulation, D: collaborative comparison, E: dual fit score). Kiran chose Option E immediately — the idea of mutual evaluation resonated with his values. He added nuance: the pager service (direct notification to Kiran) should only appear AFTER the fit score establishes mutual interest. The reverse scoring "has to be honest, not defensive."

**Reasoning:** Every other skills-matching tool treats the candidate as the subject of evaluation. Flipping the lens — showing the recruiter what Kiran would evaluate about THEIR opportunity — differentiates the feature and signals the kind of hire Kiran is. It also serves the functional gate principle: this genuinely requires the recruiter's context (their JD) to work, making identity verification natural rather than forced.

**Impact:** Technically the most complex feature in the unlock system. Needs a dedicated session to design scoring dimensions, weighting methodology, the reverse-evaluation criteria, and how to present mutual results without seeming arrogant. Sets the pattern for connected unlocks across other personas.

**Open Questions:** How to weight scoring dimensions fairly. How to handle cases where the reverse score suggests poor fit (diplomatic honesty vs. rejection risk). Whether to surface Kiran's actual non-negotiables or keep them abstract.

### Constraint Framework: 3 Per Persona, Prioritization Test
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Architecture

**Decision (proposed, not locked):** Maximum 3 unlocks per persona (2 anonymous + 1 connected). Features must pass a 3-part prioritization test: (1) Does it match the persona's primary job-to-be-done? (2) Is it differentiated from what they'd find on any portfolio site? (3) Is it authentically Kiran — could only he deliver this?

**Context:** The Evaluator brainstorm produced 6-7 feature ideas before constraints were applied. Kiran recognized the expansion problem: "I feel like we need to define a few constraints to help us play within some meaningful boundaries." He proposed working backwards from a future reality where the unlock component is live, asking what minimal set of features would be most impactful.

**Reasoning:** Constraints are generative. A 3-per-persona cap forces prioritization, which forces clarity about what matters most. The 3-part test ensures each surviving feature serves the visitor's need (job-to-be-done), stands out (differentiation), and could only come from Kiran (authenticity). Features that pass all three are the right ones; features that fail any one need redesigning or cutting.

**Impact:** If confirmed, this caps the total unlock system at 18 features (6 personas × 3 unlocks). This is manageable for design, build, and maintenance. The Inner Circle exception (no connected unlocks) means their 3 could all be anonymous.

**Open Questions:** Whether 3 is the right number or if some personas need 4. Whether the constraint applies uniformly or allows persona-specific exceptions. Kiran hasn't explicitly confirmed this constraint yet.

### Mobile & Consumer as Third Resume Lens — Career Direction Over Career History
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Career Strategy

**Decision:** The third resume lens is "Mobile & Consumer Product" — not Enterprise & Platform. Chosen to break from the banking anchor and target consumer-first tech companies on Kiran's list (Airbnb, Uber, Netflix, Disney, Meta, etc.).

**Context:** Market research validated AI PM and Growth PM as distinct hiring categories with high volume. The original third option (Product Strategy) was shown to be table stakes at VP level. Kiran then pushed: the lenses should serve where he's GOING, not where he's been. Enterprise & Platform would anchor him in fintech history. Mobile & Consumer points toward his target companies — all consumer-facing mobile-first products.

**Reasoning:** Mobile experience spans industries. Kiran has expert-level mobile depth (Wells Fargo 18M→32M MAU, first tablet apps, entire First Republic mobile experience, Avatour mobile AR/VR). This lens says "I've done this at scale in banking, and the skills transfer to any consumer product." Also opened the door to consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG) who want someone who understands consumer product at scale.

**Impact:** Resume pipeline needs 3 lens variants. The customize-resume skill can generate these from templates. The unconventional rendered page version also needs to reflect the mobile-first narrative. This is the first explicit career-positioning decision encoded in the unlock architecture — the site's features now encode Kiran's career direction, not just his career history.

**Open Questions:** Do the lens variants differ only in bullet emphasis or also in structure? Does each need its own summary paragraph?

### Pushover as CC Notification Platform — Build Services, Not Features
**Date:** 2026-04-03
**Category:** Architecture

**Decision:** The pager notification uses Pushover ($5 one-time purchase), built as a generic CC notification service — not a Fit Score-specific feature. Any CC event can trigger a push notification with configurable priority levels.

**Context:** Kiran asked about PagerDuty for real-time notifications. Claude identified it as overkill (enterprise incident management). Pushover was proposed as purpose-built for app-to-human notifications. Kiran immediately went meta: "Can I use this for CC to send me pushes for other events? Would it scale?"

**Reasoning:** Building a notification service instead of a notification feature means one implementation powers multiple use cases: mutual Fit Score matches (high priority), persona conversions (normal), resume downloads (low), testimonials (normal), engagement thresholds (normal), scheduled task completions (low). Priority levels differentiate urgency so the pager signal stays strong even as other notifications are added.

**Impact:** New CC backend feature: `notify_kiran(title, message, priority)`. Requires Pushover app purchase and API key registration. Affects command-center workstream. The Fit Score pager is the first use case but the infrastructure serves everything.

**Open Questions:** Pushover priority mapping for different CC events. Whether to add notification preferences (quiet hours, category muting). Whether to eventually expose this as a configurable notification center in the CC dashboard.

### Asymmetric Fit Score Dimensions — Five Per Direction
**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Category:** Architecture | Product Philosophy

**Decision:** The Dual Fit Score uses two asymmetric sets of 5 dimensions each. Role→Kiran dimensions are fundamentally different from Kiran→Role dimensions because the evaluation questions are different.

**Context:** Initial proposal had generic, symmetric dimensions (culture fit, growth potential, compensation, etc.). Kiran rejected this: "These are too vague. What would I actually be evaluating?" The session spent 90 minutes drilling into what actually matters when evaluating a role opportunity vs. evaluating a company.

**Reasoning:** The two directions ask different questions. Kiran→Role answers: "Can I do this job well?" (Experience & Level, Domain & Industry, Technical Depth, Core Competencies, Product Stage). Role→Kiran answers: "Is this company worth my time?" (Culture & Values, Growth Trajectory, Product Vision, Team Quality, Company Stage & Momentum). Symmetric dimensions would force false equivalence.

**Scoring specifics:**
- Percentage (0-100%) with qualitative bands: Strong (85-100%), Solid (65-84%), Partial (45-64%), Stretch (below 45%)
- "Developing" rejected (corporate performance language); "Gap" rejected (verdict language); "Partial" and "Stretch" approved
- Each dimension: score + explanation + "what would increase this"
- Vague JD handling: minimum 3 of 5 dimensions must be scorable; if not, Fenix declines and diagnoses gaps
- Narrated enrichment: Fenix shows research in real time ("Cross-referencing against Kiran's work... checking company financials... reading their technical blog...")

**Impact:** Technologically significant — requires different scoring models per direction, asymmetric weighting logic, and dimension-specific explanation templates. Sets the pattern for other personas' connected unlocks.

**Open Questions:** How to handle dimension-level conflicts (e.g., "Strong Technical Depth" but "Unclear AI ownership in JD")? How to diplomatically surface reverse-direction misalignment without seeming arrogant? Should dimensions be persona-specific or universal across all personas?

### Preferred Companies as Flat List with Bonus Logic
**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Category:** Career Strategy | Architecture

**Decision:** Maintain a single flat list of 25 target companies (no public tiering). When Fit Score detects a Preferred Company match, add a flat +5 point bonus to Kiran→Role composite. Non-preferred companies see no signal (graceful absence, not penalty).

**Context:** Kiran asked Claude to research target companies rather than rely on memory. Research produced AI-native, consumer, SaaS, fintech, growth, and consulting categories. Kiran selectively accepted suggestions (Ramp, NVIDIA, Robinhood, Bain, Canva but rejected Databricks, Revolut, Plaid). When Claude asked how to surface tiers (dream vs. stepping stone), Kiran said: "One flat list. The dimensions differentiate, not the tier."

**Reasoning:** Public tiering (dream vs. stepping stone) creates a hierarchy that weakens the authentic-evaluation positioning. A flat list says: "These 25 are strategically aligned with where I'm heading." The Fit Score dimensions handle differentiation — Anthropic might be "Strong" on Culture & Values but Airbnb might be "Solid" on Product Vision. The bonus is just +5 points, not a multiplier, and it communicates "you work for one of my target companies" without creating a prestige ranking.

**Preferred Company List (Final, 25 total):**
- **AI-Native:** Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, Apple, NVIDIA
- **Consumer & Marketplace:** Airbnb, Uber, Netflix, Disney, Meta, Snap, Canva
- **Product-Led SaaS:** Adobe, Intuit, ServiceNow, Asana, Figma, Shopify
- **Fintech:** Stripe, Block, Ramp, Robinhood
- **Growth & Mobile:** Spotify, DoorDash, Duolingo
- **Consulting:** McKinsey, BCG, Deloitte, Bain
- **General:** Microsoft

**Impact:** Fit Score frontend needs conditional logic to detect Preferred Company from recruiter input (or Fenix research). +5 point bonus is simple math. Non-preferred companies are treated as equally valid, just not on active target list.

**Open Questions:** Should "Preferred Company" be a visible label to recruiters, or kept private? How does Fenix communicate the bonus without creating prestige hierarchies? Should the list evolve over time or stay static?

### Three-Layer Video Strategy — Hero + Fenix Frame + Persona Option
**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Category:** Product Philosophy | Brand Identity

**Decision:** The site uses three distinct video layers: (1) Hero video on homepage before persona picker (AI avatar, 15-20s, 4-beat narrative), (2) Fenix text-based opening frame per persona (immediate, no friction), (3) Optional persona-specific video offered by Fenix during conversation.

**Context:** Initial conversation was about "should the hero video include Kiran's face?" The discovery was that video serves multiple moments, each with different purposes. Layer 1 sets tone globally. Layer 2 begins the persona-specific relationship. Layer 3 offers deeper engagement optionally.

**Reasoning:** Each layer has a distinct purpose and audience mindset:
- **Layer 1 (Hero):** Visitor is pre-persona, needs context, attention span is short, autopay with auto-mute after 3s
- **Layer 2 (Fenix frame):** Visitor has selected a persona, ready for deeper engagement, text is fast and scannable, sets conversation tone
- **Layer 3 (Persona video):** Visitor has engaged with Fenix, may want visual/emotional deepening, optional (graceful absence if skipped)

Avatar choice: AI-generated, not Kiran's face. Two-fold: (1) Privacy boundary — non-digital life stays private, site is the product, (2) Appearance bias elimination — removes unconscious evaluation based on age, ethnicity, gender presentation, attractiveness.

**Impact:** Requires three distinct assets (hero video, 5 persona text frames, 5+ persona video variants). High-fingerprint moments requiring careful sourcing. Claude recommends external tools for avatar generation (Midjourney, custom avatar service) and hero video production (Runway, Pika).

**Open Questions:** Which avatar tool gives the most intentional, cohesive result? Should persona videos be animated or live-action? How long should each persona video be? Should they be Fenix (avatar) or Kiran (voiceover)?

### Fenix Opening Frame — Persona-Specific Textual Introduction
**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Category:** Product Philosophy | Brand Identity

**Decision:** Each persona's unlock component begins with a Fenix opening frame — persona-specific text that explains what the visitor will experience, what Fenix brings, and why this site is different. Not a generic greeting; a contextual, relational introduction.

**Context:** The opening frame evolved from Fenix's floating action button introduction to a proper first moment inside the unlock module. For Evaluator (recruiter), it needed to explain the Fit Score, the uniqueness of mutual evaluation, and what Fenix brings to the conversation.

**Fenix Opening Frame (Evaluator — Locked):**
> "Quick context before we start. This isn't a portfolio site. It's a product Kiran built. The resume comes in three versions, each tuned to a different kind of search. There's a Fit Score that evaluates both directions — whether Kiran fits the role and whether the role fits Kiran. And I'm not a template chatbot. I've been trained on Kiran's actual work, his decisions, and how he thinks. This site isn't designed for a 30-second skim. But every minute you spend here will surface insights you'd otherwise spend weeks piecing together. The more you experience the uniqueness of this site, the more you understand about how Kiran thinks and works. I'm here to help you focus on what matters to you."

**Key language decisions:**
- "It's a product Kiran built" — Site itself is evidence, not marketing asset
- "Evaluates both directions" — Mutual evaluation stated upfront
- "I'm not a template chatbot" — Differentiates Fenix from generic AI
- "The more you experience the uniqueness..." — Contributed by Kiran; emphasizes experience over metrics
- No "pitch" language — Rejected as too salesy; framing is informational, not persuasive

**Reasoning:** This frame sets the entire tone for the persona's experience. It's Fenix's first voice, Kiran's first impression, and the positioning statement all in one. Every line serves to establish: this is different, personalized, relational, worth your time. The Evaluator frame emphasizes mutual evaluation and intellectual honesty; other personas will emphasize different aspects (Technologist: depth and decision-making; Seeker: problem-solving partnership; Practitioner: portfolio evidence).

**Fenix pronouns:** He/him throughout (not she/her, corrected during session).

**Impact:** Requires 5 additional frames for other personas (Technologist, Practitioner, Seeker, Learner, Inner Circle), each with persona-specific language and focus. Sets the conversational tone that carries through Fenix pills and open-ended interaction.

**Open Questions:** How much variation should exist between persona frames? Should they all hit the same key points (mutual evaluation, non-generic, relational) or emphasize entirely different themes? Should the Inner Circle frame address the "no connected unlock" design?

### Fourth Pill: "Give Me a Quick Tour"
**Date:** 2026-04-04
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** The persona picker's fourth pill changes from "Something else" to "Give me a quick tour." This navigates to the Under the Hood page with Fenix providing persona-aware commentary on the site's architecture and philosophy.

**Context:** "Something else" was too vague and offered no clear outcome. Kiran wanted a pill that could accommodate visitors who wanted context but not the structured unlock options. The tour satisfies this: show me how this site is built, what decisions were made, why it's different.

**Reasoning:** The site's architecture is part of the product story. Showing a visitor "under the hood" (the term survived, though "behind the scenes" was rejected as too techy) demonstrates the thinking, the intentionality, the care. For the Technologist persona, this is especially valuable — they evaluate products by their structure, not their surface. For others, it's a way to deepen understanding without a transactional gate.

**Fenix tour mode:** Persona-aware. The Technologist's tour commentary emphasizes architecture and technical decisions. The Practitioner's emphasizes product thinking. The Seeker's emphasizes how Kiran solves problems. Same Under the Hood page, different narrative angles.

**Impact:** Requires Under the Hood page (content + structure) and Fenix system prompt modes for persona-aware commentary. Removes the vague "Something else" and replaces it with a clear user journey.

**Open Questions:** What exactly goes in the Under the Hood page? Technical diagrams, decision rationales, build process, philosophy? Should it be one page or multiple? Should Fenix guide through a specific path or offer choices?
