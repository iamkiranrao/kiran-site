# Strategic Decisions — March 24, 2026 (Continuation Session)

### Work Card Lineup Locked: The Relational Frame Wins
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** LOCKED. The work card lineup shifts from 8 cards (with 4 weak) to 8 cards (all aligned to both the Evaluator persona and the relational authenticity principle).

**Old lineup:** Career Highlights, How I'd've Built It, MadLab, Studio, Blog & Podcast, Learning & Certifications, Causes, Store

**New lineup:** Career Highlights, How I'd've Built It, MadLab (absorbs Studio), Blog & Podcast, Under the Hood, Frameworks & Thinking Tools, /Now, Learn With Me

**Reasoning:** The original evaluation frame (Persona Pull, Differentiation, JTBD) was correct but incomplete. It flagged weak cards but didn't ask: "weak by what standard?" When that standard was reframed from "does this help a hiring manager decide" to "does this authentically represent how I think and grow," every weak card either improved or was replaced with something stronger.

- **Career Highlights** stays because it's commodity-essential for the Evaluator persona AND authentically represents professional growth.
- **How I'd've Built It** stays as the flagship because it serves both frames equally — hiring managers want to see thinking, and it authentically shows Kiran's analysis.
- **MadLab absorbs Studio** because visitors don't care whether prototypes are functional or creative — they care about independent building. The card description expands to include "Prototypes, AI experiments, creative tools — the stuff I build when nobody's watching."
- **Blog & Podcast** stays as the content anchor.
- **Under the Hood** replaces Store because it demonstrates the exact skill the site promises to showcase: building with AI, architectural thinking, tool selection. Maximum differentiation — no other portfolio shows how it was built.
- **Frameworks & Thinking Tools** replaces a repositioned Learning & Certifications because practitioners actually share frameworks and reuse mental models. This is active engagement, not passive credentialing. It signals "here's how I think" rather than "look at my credentials."
- **/Now** (Derek Sivers-inspired) replaces Causes because it serves the relational promise ("this is my home on the internet") while being authentic and low-friction. Inner Circle gets a meaningful unlock, Seeker gets transparency.
- **Learn With Me** (active mentorship, ADPList, learning-in-public) replaces the credential-focused card because it's relational, not transactional.

**Impact:** All eight cards now pass both evaluation lenses. The card lineup clearly articulates "this site is a workshop, not a resume." This eliminates the tension between serving hiring managers and staying authentic. They're not mutually exclusive — they're just being asked through different frames.

**Open Questions:** Three of four new cards require content that doesn't exist yet (architecture docs, frameworks, /now page). What's the content creation roadmap? Should all content be ready before cards ship, or can cards go up with "coming soon" states?

### Studio and MadLab: The Absorption Decision
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Category:** Architecture

**Decision:** LOCKED. Studio is not preserved as a separate card. Its content is absorbed into MadLab with expanded copy and broader categorization.

**Reasoning:** The distinction between "AI art/video/creative" (Studio) and "prototypes/apps/experiments" (MadLab) is meaningful to the creator but not to the visitor. Both answer the same question from the visitor's perspective: "What does Kiran build independently?" Separating them creates two mediocre cards instead of one strong one. Absorbing Studio into MadLab with explicit mention of creative experiments strengthens the differentiation angle without needing a separate card.

**Copy for absorbed MadLab:** "Prototypes, AI experiments, creative tools — the stuff I build when nobody's watching."

**Impact:** Reduces card count from 9 to 8 (one less button to evaluate). Increases clarity of what MadLab represents. Frees up the card slot for either Under the Hood or another high-differentiation option.

### Under the Hood: Meta-narrative as Proof Point
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** LOCKED. A new card showcasing the architecture and build process of kiranrao.ai itself replaces the Store card.

**Reasoning:** The site's thesis is "this is built with AI, and the process demonstrates capability." Under the Hood delivers directly on that promise. It shows: (1) tool selection and integration, (2) architectural decisions and tradeoffs, (3) the collaboration process between human and AI. No traditional portfolio shows this. It's maximum differentiation.

**Content required:** Architecture decision records (Cloudflare Workers, Fenix AI integration, persona personalization system), tool documentation (Midjourney, Runway, Claude, etc.), build process narrative, what was automated vs. what was manual. Some exists; more needs to be written.

**Serves:** Technologist (primary), Practitioner (secondary), Learner (secondary)

**Impact:** Creates accountability for the site's implicit promise to be transparent about its own building process. The card can't exist without the documentation. This is good pressure — it forces the documentation work upfront.

### Frameworks & Thinking Tools: Active Sharing Replaces Passive Credentialing
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** LOCKED. Learning & Certifications is repositioned and renamed as Frameworks & Thinking Tools.

**Previous positioning:** Passive list of credentials and certifications (undermined the "work speaks for itself" principle)

**New positioning:** Active sharing of reusable decision frameworks, mental models, and analysis templates that practitioners and leaders actually use. Learning-in-public signal. Evidence of how Kiran thinks through hard problems.

**Reasoning:** Practitioners (product, design, data) share useful frameworks with their teams and communities. This isn't about showing credentials — it's about showing thinking as a reusable artifact. Research from product leadership communities (Reforge, Silicon Valley Product Group, Lenny's Product Newsletter) shows that reusable frameworks and templates get shared and cited more than credentials ever do. This card signals "I think in ways worth adopting" rather than "I have certifications."

**Content required:** A curated collection of frameworks Kiran has developed or refined. Could include: hiring frameworks, product thinking checklists, design evaluation templates, decision-making matrices, etc.

**Serves:** Practitioner (primary), Seeker (secondary), Learner (secondary)

**Impact:** Transforms a weak card into a strong one by changing what it's asking. The content work is significant (requires curating and documenting frameworks), but the value is real.

### /Now: Relational Transparency
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Category:** Brand/Identity

**Decision:** LOCKED. A Derek Sivers-style "what I'm working on right now" page replaces Causes.

**Reasoning:** The Causes card (check if Kiran is charitable) had zero persona alignment. But the *intent* behind including it was relational — showing that the person behind the site cares about things beyond work. A /Now page serves that intent infinitely better. It signals "this is my home on the internet" by being transparent about what's actually claiming my attention right now. It's authentic, it's low friction, and it creates recurring reasons for people to come back.

**Reference:** nownownow.com movement (Derek Sivers' site tracking what people are doing now)

**Content required:** A current status of projects, priorities, learning, and focus. Can be updated monthly. Very low friction.

**Serves:** Inner Circle (primary — they get to see what Kiran is actually focused on), Seeker (secondary — transparency about availability and direction), Evaluator (secondary — signals active building, not static portfolio)

**Impact:** Small content effort with disproportionate relational payoff. This card should go live early.

### Learn With Me: Redefining the Learning Signal
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** LOCKED. A new card emphasizing active mentorship and learning-in-public replaces the passive credentials positioning.

**What it includes:** (1) ADPList integration for mentorship booking, (2) learning philosophy articulation, (3) active case studies of mentees Kiran has worked with, (4) Fenix mode for mentorship conversations

**Reasoning:** The old Learning & Certifications card tried to build credibility through credentials. This card builds credibility through mentorship. It signals "I help people grow" — which is higher-value than "I have certificates." The ADPList integration makes it functional, not just aspirational. A learner visiting the site gets immediate access to book mentorship, not a list of courses taken.

**Content required:** ADPList profile, mentorship philosophy doc, case studies or examples of mentees, Fenix backend integration for mentorship mode

**Serves:** Learner (primary), Practitioner (secondary — people learning to lead), Inner Circle (secondary)

**Impact:** Creates a functional unlock that serves the Learner persona specifically. This is Track 2 content (functional per-persona unlock), but the card itself lives at the top level. Good integration opportunity.

### The Evaluator Persona: Four Cards Serve It Directly
**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Category:** Product Philosophy

**Decision:** NOT A PROBLEM. The new lineup serves the Evaluator persona through four cards (Career Highlights, How I'd've Built It, Under the Hood, Frameworks & Thinking Tools), each demonstrating different dimensions of capability.

**Reasoning:** The original concern was that the new lineup leaned toward "workshop" and away from "portfolio," potentially underserving the Evaluator persona (recruiters, hiring managers) who came to assess hiring fit. But the new four-card cluster for Evaluator is stronger than any single transactional signal:

1. **Career Highlights** — Credentials and track record (commodity but necessary)
2. **How I'd've Built It** — Product thinking and judgment (core differentiator)
3. **Under the Hood** — Technical capability and AI collaboration (proof of the promise)
4. **Frameworks & Thinking Tools** — Systems thinking and reusable intelligence (leadership signal)

These four cards comprehensively answer "can this person do the work?" They're stronger together than the original lineup was.

**Impact:** Eliminates the false tension between relational authenticity and hiring manager conversion. The cards serve both.
