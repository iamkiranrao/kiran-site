## The Card Strategy: From Weak-Four to Reframed-Eight

The work card analysis was the technical heart of today. Started with the framework from the previous session:

- **Persona Pull** — Does this card serve the six personas?
- **Differentiation** — Is it distinctive or commodity?
- **JTBD Completion** — Does it help a visitor accomplish what they came for?

Four cards scored weak: Store, Causes, Learning & Certifications, Studio. Four were proposed as replacements: Under the Hood, Frameworks & Tools, /Now, Learn With Me.

But the real work happened when Kiran pressure-tested his own assumptions. When challenged on Learning & Certifications, he recognized the card wasn't inherently weak — it was being framed wrong. "Learning & Certifications" (passive list) contradicts the site's thesis. But "Learn With Me" (active mentorship) signals learning-in-public and embodies the relational experience the site promises. The card wasn't the problem; the positioning was.

This reframing changes the entire card evaluation. Instead of cutting cards that don't serve the job-search persona, the question became: what content authentically represents how I think and grow? That's a relational frame, not a transactional one. And when you ask that question, the lineup reorganizes.

**Proposed final lineup:**
- Career Highlights (stays — commodity but essential for Evaluator)
- How I'd've Built It (stays — flagship differentiator, serves teardown GTM)
- MadLab (absorbs Studio — cleaner, broader description)
- Blog & Podcast (stays — content anchor)
- Under the Hood (replaces Store — meta-narrative of building with AI)
- Frameworks & Thinking Tools (replaces Learning & Certs positioning — active sharing)
- /Now (replaces Causes — relational, authentic signal)
- Learn With Me (new — active mentorship, engagement)

Eight cards. Dramatically higher clarity on what they serve. Each one now passes both the Evaluator lens (would this catch a hiring manager's eye?) AND the relational lens (does this authentically represent how Kiran thinks?).

## Evening Session: From Debugging to New Sections

The final session of this marathon day brought the persona accent system to completion and added two new homepage sections that weren't in the original gameplan.

### The Inner Stroke Saga Concludes

The `::after` pseudo-element approach for avatar inner strokes was architecturally correct but had two implementation bugs that cascaded:

1. **`overflow: hidden` on wrapper elements** — This was clipping the `::after` at the boundary. The pseudo-element existed (confirmed via `getComputedStyle`), had the right properties, but its border was being cut off at the wrapper's clip edge. Removing `overflow: hidden` fixed the rendering.

2. **Missing `border-radius: 50%` on the image** — With `overflow: hidden` removed, the image was no longer being clipped into a circle by its parent. The image itself needed `border-radius: 50%`. This was a dependency that Claude should have anticipated — if you remove the parent's clip, the child needs to clip itself.

The fix required two commits (`184f267` and `17b9799`) when it should have been one. The diagnostic approach was sound — using `getComputedStyle` to verify the pseudo-element existed, then injecting a debug red border to prove the mechanism worked — but the root cause analysis was slow. The interaction between `overflow`, `border-radius`, and `::after` positioning is well-documented CSS behavior.

**Lesson for the codebase:** When using `::after` overlays on image containers, the image needs explicit `border-radius` matching the container. Don't rely on parent `overflow: hidden` for visual clipping when you need pseudo-elements to render at the boundary.

### Two New Sections: C10 and C11

The gameplan audit revealed that the Claude Track was effectively complete — all original C1-C9 items shipped. During the review, Kiran identified two missing sections:

**C10: Manifesto Video** — A full-width cinematic placeholder between About and the work section. 21:9 aspect ratio (scaling to 16:9 on mobile), dark canvas with gradient overlay, play button SVG, and italic Playfair "Why this site exists." tagline. The persona accent threads through the top/bottom borders at 30% mix.

The design decision here was to make it feel like a movie theater — dark, wide, centered focus. The play button is deliberately understated (0.6 opacity, only popping on hover). The tagline uses Playfair Display italic, which is the site's serif accent font, creating a visual break from the Inter body text.

**C11: By the Numbers** — A 6-card metric grid below the manifesto. Cards have accent-colored values, bordered containers with hover glow, and responsive layout (6-col → 3-col → 2-col). Currently all placeholders — the interesting design challenge will be making the metrics persona-specific. An Evaluator cares about different proof points than a Technologist.

Both sections slot between About and the Work section, creating a narrative flow: who I am → why this exists → here's the evidence → now explore.
