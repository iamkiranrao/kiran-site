IT & EQUIPMENT SETUP POLICY
NovaTech Corporation Information Technology

OVERVIEW

This document outlines the process for IT and equipment provisioning for new employees and provides guidance on technology security requirements at NovaTech Corporation.

LAPTOP OPTIONS & PROVISIONING

All employees receive a company laptop upon their first day of employment. Employees have the choice between two laptop options:

MacBook Pro: 14-inch or 16-inch display, M3 Pro/Max chip, 16GB or 32GB RAM (as per role requirements), 512GB SSD storage. MacBook Pro models are typically configured with 8-core CPU, 10-core GPU, and come preloaded with NovaTech-standard software configurations.

Lenovo ThinkPad: X1 Carbon (14th Gen), 16GB or 32GB RAM (as per role requirements), 512GB SSD storage. ThinkPad models are typically configured with Intel Core i7 processor, integrated graphics, and come preloaded with Windows 11 Pro and NovaTech standard applications.

Your choice of laptop should be communicated to the IT team during onboarding. The IT team will order your selected machine, and you will receive it by your first day or within the first week of employment. All laptops are configured by NovaTech's IT department before being provided to ensure security compliance and software availability.

Both laptop options have equivalent specifications and capabilities for our standard development and office work. GPU-intensive roles (e.g., video production, machine learning) may be provided upgraded specifications at manager discretion.

OKTA SINGLE SIGN-ON (SSO)

All NovaTech employees use Okta for centralized identity and access management. Your Okta account is created during onboarding and enables seamless, secure authentication across all NovaTech systems.

Upon first login, you will be required to set up multi-factor authentication (MFA). Okta will prompt you to register a device (mobile phone or authenticator app). We recommend using the Okta Verify mobile app or Google Authenticator for MFA.

Your Okta username is typically firstname.lastname@novatech.com. This credential provides access to:
- Email (Gmail via Google Workspace)
- Slack
- GitHub (enterprise account)
- Notion (internal documentation)
- All SaaS applications and cloud services
- VPN and remote access systems

Keep your Okta password secure and never share it. If you forget your password, visit the Okta login page and click "Forgot Password?" to reset it.

SLACK ACCESS & COMMUNICATION

Slack is NovaTech's primary communication platform. Your Slack account is created on day one and you will automatically be added to:

- #general: Company-wide announcements and discussion
- Your department or team channels (e.g., #engineering, #product, #sales)
- #onboarding: Resources and support for new employees
- #announcements: Official company announcements and policies
- #random: Casual team conversation

Download the Slack app on your laptop and mobile device for seamless communication. You may also access Slack through your web browser at https://novatech.slack.com.

Slack is our primary channel for immediate communication, questions, and team collaboration. For non-urgent requests, expect a response within 1-2 business hours. For urgent matters, you can contact your manager directly.

NOTION DOCUMENTATION & WIKIS

Notion is our company knowledge management and documentation platform. Upon onboarding, you will be given access to:

- Engineering Wiki: System architecture, deployment processes, code standards
- Product Documentation: Product roadmaps, feature specifications, requirements
- HR Documentation: Policies, benefits guides, onboarding materials
- Team Spaces: Department-specific documentation and meeting notes
- Company Information: Meeting schedules, team directories, org charts

Your manager will provide you with a guided tour of Notion during your first week. Save the main Notion workspace URL to your browser bookmarks for easy access.

GITHUB ACCESS

All engineers and technical staff receive access to NovaTech's GitHub enterprise organization. Your GitHub account should be created with your personal GitHub account, then linked to the NovaTech organization.

Upon joining, you will be added to relevant repositories based on your team and role. Your GitHub username will be displayed in Okta during onboarding—use this to request access to required repositories.

GitHub access is managed through SSO authentication via Okta. You will not need to maintain a separate GitHub password; Okta login grants you access to our GitHub organization. For specific repository access questions, contact your engineering manager or the DevOps team.

VPN SETUP & REMOTE ACCESS

All NovaTech employees have access to VPN (Virtual Private Network) to enable secure remote work. VPN setup is completed during your first day IT onboarding session.

VPN Connection Details:
- VPN Platform: Okta Advanced Server Access (ASA) or equivalent
- Authentication: Okta SSO credentials + MFA
- Required on: All remote work sessions, when accessing sensitive systems from non-NovaTech networks
- Connection Instructions: Provided during IT setup, also documented in Notion's IT Wiki

To connect to the VPN, download the required VPN client (Okta ASA client or similar), enter your Okta credentials, and complete MFA authentication. Once connected, you will have secure access to internal systems, file shares, and development environments.

PASSWORD POLICY & SECURITY

All NovaTech employees must adhere to the following password policy:

- Minimum 12 characters in length
- Must include uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters
- Cannot contain your username or common dictionary words
- Must be changed at least annually (typically January)
- Previous 5 passwords cannot be reused
- Account lockout after 5 failed login attempts (30-minute lockout period)
- Passwords must never be shared with colleagues, even temporarily

Password resets are managed through Okta's self-service password reset portal. If you forget your password, visit https://novatech.okta.com and click "Forgot Password?"

MULTI-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION (MFA) REQUIREMENT

Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all NovaTech employees and non-negotiable. MFA must be configured during your first IT setup session.

Supported MFA Methods:
- Okta Verify App (recommended): Mobile push notifications for verification
- Google Authenticator: Time-based one-time passwords (TOTP)
- Authy: Backup MFA app with multi-device sync
- Hardware Security Key: YubiKey or similar (for high-security roles)

You must configure at least one MFA method. Many employees configure two methods for backup purposes. MFA is required for:
- Initial Okta login
- VPN access
- GitHub access
- Access to sensitive internal systems

If you lose access to your MFA device, contact IT immediately at it-support@novatech.com. IT will verify your identity through alternative means and temporarily disable MFA while you set up a replacement device.

DEVICE SECURITY & ENCRYPTION

All company laptops must meet the following security requirements:

- FileVault encryption (macOS) or BitLocker (Windows): Enabled on all devices
- Automatic screen lock: After 15 minutes of inactivity
- Antivirus/Malware Protection: Installed and kept current
- Operating System Updates: Applied automatically or manually within 48 hours of availability
- Disk encryption passwords: Stored securely and never shared

Failure to maintain device security compliance may result in suspension of access to NovaTech systems.

EQUIPMENT RETURN & OFF-BOARDING

When an employee departs NovaTech, all company equipment must be returned within 2 business days of the final work day:
- Laptop and charger
- Mobile phone and accessories
- Security keys, access badges
- Any other company property

Equipment should be returned to the IT department at your office location or arranged for pick-up. Failure to return equipment will result in costs being deducted from final pay.
