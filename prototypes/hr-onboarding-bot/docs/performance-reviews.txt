PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT & EMPLOYEE GROWTH
NovaTech Corporation Performance Review System

OVERVIEW

NovaTech Corporation is committed to supporting employee development, growth, and high performance. This policy outlines our comprehensive approach to performance management, including regular feedback, formal reviews, and career advancement.

PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT PHILOSOPHY

We believe that:
- Regular, constructive feedback is more valuable than annual reviews alone
- Employees and managers share responsibility for development and growth
- Clear expectations and transparent communication drive performance
- Recognition of achievements motivates and retains top talent
- Continuous learning and improvement are cultural values

Performance management at NovaTech is a collaborative process, not a one-directional evaluation. Managers are trained to support employee development, provide coaching, and remove barriers to success.

QUARTERLY CHECK-INS

All employees participate in quarterly check-in conversations with their manager at the end of each quarter (typically March, June, September, December). These informal, forward-looking discussions are designed to:

- Review progress on current projects and goals
- Provide feedback on performance and development areas
- Discuss career aspirations and learning goals
- Remove obstacles and provide necessary support
- Align on priorities for the upcoming quarter
- Recognize contributions and achievements

Quarterly check-ins are lighter-weight than annual reviews and focus on continuous feedback rather than evaluation. They typically take 30-45 minutes and are documented briefly in your performance file.

Employees are encouraged to bring their own feedback, questions, and concerns to these discussions. Quarterly check-ins are opportunities for dialogue, not lectures.

ANNUAL PERFORMANCE REVIEWS

All employees complete a formal annual performance review in November. The annual review process includes:

Self-Assessment: Employees complete a self-assessment form evaluating their own performance against goals, behavior standards, and competencies. This typically takes 45-60 minutes.

Manager Review: Your direct manager completes an assessment based on performance observations, feedback from colleagues, and goal achievement. Managers are trained to provide specific examples and evidence.

360-Degree Feedback: Employees receive feedback from peers, direct reports (if applicable), and cross-functional colleagues. Multiple perspectives help provide balanced, comprehensive feedback.

Review Meeting: Your manager will schedule a meeting to discuss the review findings, provide feedback, and discuss development plans. This meeting typically takes 60 minutes.

Documentation: The annual review is documented in your personnel file and used for determining salary adjustments, promotions, and development planning.

Annual reviews are held consistently in November to allow time for year-end reflection and planning. Exceptions for extended leave are accommodated.

PERFORMANCE RATING SCALE

Employees are typically rated on the following 5-point scale during annual reviews:

5 - Exceptional Performer: Consistently exceeds expectations, demonstrates mastery, mentors others, drives innovation. Top 10% of performers. Eligible for promotion and above-market salary adjustments.

4 - Strong Performer: Consistently meets and sometimes exceeds expectations. Performs core responsibilities at a high standard. Solid contributor to team. Eligible for promotion and market-rate salary adjustments.

3 - Meets Expectations: Meets job expectations consistently and performs core responsibilities competently. Meets established performance standards. Eligible for standard cost-of-living salary adjustments.

2 - Needs Improvement: Does not consistently meet job expectations. Performance falls below acceptable standards in some areas. Performance improvement plan (PIP) may be recommended. Limited salary adjustment eligibility.

1 - Does Not Meet Expectations: Significantly below performance expectations and job requirements. Failure to meet performance standards across multiple areas. Performance improvement plan (PIP) required. No salary adjustment; potential for termination.

Ratings 4-5 are typically reserved for 30-40% of the population across the company. Ratings are calibrated annually to ensure consistency across departments.

PROMOTION CRITERIA

Employees interested in advancement should discuss career goals with their managers early. Promotion decisions are based on demonstrated performance in current role, readiness for increased responsibility, and alignment with business needs.

General Promotion Criteria:
- Consistent performance rating of 4 or higher for at least 12-18 months in current role
- Demonstrated mastery of core competencies required for current role
- Successful completion of projects or initiatives relevant to higher-level role
- Clear communication of growth aspirations and willingness to develop
- Positive feedback from peers, team members, and cross-functional partners
- Cultural alignment with NovaTech values
- Readiness for increased scope, complexity, or people management responsibilities

Promotion Processes:
- Typically discussed during annual review or quarterly check-ins
- Career development conversation between employee and manager outlining a promotion pathway
- Gap analysis identifying skills, experience, or behaviors to develop
- Opportunities to take on stretch projects or expanded responsibilities
- Review by department director and HR to assess readiness and organizational fit
- Promotion decisions communicated with offer details (title, compensation, start date)

Promotions typically take effect on the first of the month following approval.

COMPETENCY FRAMEWORK

NovaTech uses a competency framework to assess performance and guide development. Core competencies include:

Technical Excellence: Expertise in your domain; continuous learning and skill development; quality-focused execution.

Collaboration: Working effectively with others; supporting team goals; communicating clearly; building relationships.

Initiative: Taking ownership; identifying and solving problems; driving improvement; going beyond baseline expectations.

Adaptability: Responding effectively to change; flexibility in approach; resilience; learning from challenges.

Leadership: (for managers and leaders) Developing others; providing clear direction; making sound decisions; role modeling values.

Professionalism: Integrity; accountability; reliability; professional communication; respecting confidentiality.

During annual reviews, managers assess performance on these competencies to provide holistic feedback. Development conversations focus on strengthening key competencies for current role and those needed for future advancement.

360-DEGREE FEEDBACK PROCESS

To provide balanced, comprehensive feedback, NovaTech uses 360-degree feedback as part of the annual review process. This includes input from:

- Your direct manager (mandatory)
- Peers and colleagues (2-4 people typically)
- Direct reports (if you manage people)
- Cross-functional partners (if applicable)
- Self-assessment from you

Feedback is collected anonymously through a third-party survey platform. Written feedback and rating scores are aggregated and shared with your manager, who discusses findings with you during the annual review meeting.

360-degree feedback is confidential and is not viewed by executives or others outside your direct management chain. The purpose is development and growth, not evaluation for disciplinary purposes.

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS (PIP)

If an employee receives a performance rating of 2 or below, or if performance concerns emerge outside of annual reviews, a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) may be initiated.

PIP Structure:
- Clear definition of performance gaps and expected standards
- Specific, measurable, achievable improvement goals
- Timeline (typically 30, 60, or 90 days depending on severity)
- Support and resources provided (coaching, training, mentoring, etc.)
- Regular check-in meetings (typically weekly or bi-weekly)
- Clear success criteria and evaluation process

PIP Expectations:
- The employee must demonstrate genuine commitment to improvement
- The manager will provide coaching, support, and resources
- Progress will be monitored and documented regularly
- Honest feedback will be provided throughout the PIP period

PIP Outcomes:
- Successful Completion: Employee returns to standard performance management if goals are met
- Continuation: If partial progress is made, PIP may be extended
- Termination: If goals are not met at end of PIP period, termination of employment may result

Employees on a PIP should be aware that their employment with NovaTech is at risk. However, the PIP process is designed to provide genuine opportunity for improvement with support.

Employees on a PIP have the right to:
- Receive a written copy of the PIP with clear goals and timelines
- Attend all check-in meetings and provide their own perspective
- Request HR involvement if concerns about process fairness arise
- Appeal termination decisions if employment is ended following PIP

COMPENSATION ADJUSTMENTS

Annual salary adjustments are determined based on multiple factors:

- Performance rating (primary factor)
- Promotion or role change
- Market rate benchmarking
- Company financial performance
- Budget constraints
- Tenure and experience in role
- Geographic location

Typical salary adjustment ranges by performance rating:
- Rating 5 (Exceptional): 5-8% increase
- Rating 4 (Strong): 3-5% increase
- Rating 3 (Meets Expectations): 1-3% increase
- Rating 2 (Needs Improvement): 0% or below-inflation increase
- Rating 1 (Does Not Meet): 0% increase; PIP required

Salary adjustments are typically communicated in December and take effect January 1st. Promotions receive adjustments at the time of promotion.

CAREER DEVELOPMENT & LEARNING

NovaTech supports career development through:

- Annual $2,000 learning and development budget (see Benefits Policy)
- Internal mentorship programs matching junior employees with experienced colleagues
- Leadership development programs for high-potential employees
- Tuition reimbursement for relevant degree or certification programs (up to $5,000/year with approval)
- Conference and workshop attendance budgets
- Internal mobility and cross-team project opportunities
- Regular career conversations during check-ins and reviews

Employees are encouraged to take ownership of their development in partnership with their managers.

FEEDBACK & RECOGNITION

Continuous feedback is valued at NovaTech. In addition to formal reviews, employees should expect:

- Regular informal feedback from managers (weekly or as-needed)
- Peer recognition through Slack channels or team meetings
- Spotlight on achievements in team or company meetings
- Written documentation of accomplishments in performance files
- Public recognition for significant contributions or innovations

Managers are expected to provide timely, specific, constructive feedback. Feedback should be balanced, recognizing strengths while addressing growth areas.

APPEALS & DISPUTES

If an employee believes a performance rating or review is unfair or inaccurate, they may appeal within 5 business days of receiving the review. Appeals should be submitted in writing to the HR department outlining the specific concerns.

HR will review the appeal, consider additional information if provided, and may request input from the employee's manager and HR business partner. A resolution will be communicated within 10 business days.

Appeals do not typically result in rating changes unless clear evidence of process errors or factual inaccuracies is found. However, the appeals process provides an opportunity for dialogue and clarification.

CONFIDENTIALITY

Performance reviews and feedback are confidential. Information about ratings, feedback, and promotion discussions should not be shared beyond those with legitimate business need to know. Employees should not disclose others' performance ratings or reviews.
