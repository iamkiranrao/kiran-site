Scannibal

An iOS app with a butler concierge who scans what you eat, buy, grow, and put on your skin — then tells you what's safe and what's not. Personalised to your allergies, pets, medications, and dietary restrictions. 100% of net proceeds go to hunger relief and animal welfare.

The Product

Scannibal started with a simple question: why do you have to read the entire back of a food package to figure out if it's safe for you? If you've got a tree nut allergy, you shouldn't need to squint at an ingredients list in a grocery aisle. If your dog is with you at a farmer's market, you shouldn't have to google whether grapes are toxic to dogs while the vendor waits.

The app puts a camera-first scanner in your pocket that cross-references what it sees against your personal safety profile. You set up your allergies, your pet types, your skin conditions, your medications — once. After that, every scan is personalised. Point the camera at a restaurant menu, a supplement bottle, a skincare label, a piece of produce, or a plant in someone's yard, and the butler tells you exactly what to watch out for and why.

There are 8 scan modes, each with its own AI prompt engineered for that specific domain. Menu Analysis checks dishes against your dietary restrictions including religious laws (kosher, halal, Hindu vegetarian, Jain, Buddhist). Pet Toxicity screens foods and plants against your specific pet types. The Grocery Decoder catches hidden sugars, misleading health claims, and does the serving-size math for you. Skincare Scanner flags comedogenic ingredients and conflicts with your current routine. Supplement Checker cross-references against your medications for dangerous interactions. Produce Picker tells you how to select the best fruit or vegetable and how to store it. Grow It Yourself gives you a planting guide personalised to your climate zone and space.

The Eight Modes

Each mode has its own Gemini prompt, profile type, and domain-specific knowledge base. They share a common scanning UI but produce very different analyses.

System Architecture

The app is built with Expo and React Native, targeting iOS first. All scan processing happens server-side — the phone captures a photo, sends it to a Vercel serverless function, which forwards it to Google's Gemini 2.5 Flash model along with the user's safety profile and the mode-specific prompt. The response comes back as structured JSON that the app renders into a forensic-themed results screen.

Why Expo over bare React Native? Scannibal doesn't need native modules that Expo can't handle. The camera, image picker, haptics, and local storage all work through Expo's managed workflow. In exchange, you get EAS Build (cloud builds without Xcode), over-the-air updates for quick fixes, and a single command to submit to the App Store. The tradeoff is less control over native code — worth it for a v1 that needs to ship fast.

The Charity Mission

100% of net proceeds from Scannibal's paid tier go to two charities: Feeding America (hunger relief) and Best Friends Animal Society (animal welfare). Users choose which cause their subscription supports. The fund is named after Kiran's daughter — The DIA Fund: Dream. Inspire. Advance. — designed to grow with her from age 4 through adulthood as a documented legacy of giving.

The free beta launches first with 50 scans per day. The paid Supporter tier ($2.99/month) unlocks unlimited scans and voice narration. Transparency reports are published quarterly, even when the numbers are small.
