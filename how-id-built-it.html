How I'd've Built It

Product teardowns and redesigns of the apps we all use

I pick apart products I use daily - what works, what doesn't, and what I'd do differently. Each teardown walks through user research, a keep/kill/build analysis, redesign proposals, and a business case. Not armchair quarterbacking - real product thinking with real constraints.

I do these teardowns to sharpen how I think about product, from real user pain to a defensible redesign. Here’s how each one unfolds.

1
→
Real Pain Points

Reddit, App Store reviews, support forums. Verified complaints from the last 2 years.

"If the pain isn't real, the solution doesn't matter."

2
→
Persona & Journey

Who’s most affected? Map their emotional journey. Find where frustration peaks.

“Good products are built for someone specific.”

3
↓
Jobs to Be Done

What is the user actually trying to accomplish? Reframe from features to outcomes.

“Users don’t want features. They want progress.”

4
←
Keep / Kill / Build

What works, what’s broken, what’s missing. The strategic filter before design.

“Redesign isn’t starting over. It’s knowing what to protect.”

5
←
Wireframe Redesign

Low-fi wireframes showing structural changes. Not pixel-perfect mockups.

“Structure first. Polish later.”

6
Business Case & KPIs

Tie every change to a measurable outcome. Retention, NPS, revenue impact.

“If you can’t measure it, you can’t justify it.”
