2012–2016

Mobile becomes the primary computing platform.

AVP, Digital Product, Auth & Mobile · Wells Fargo
2012
Smartphones overtake feature phones. Mobile banking is still a novelty. Most banks are debating whether to build native apps at all.
Mobile
Mobile & Tablet App Launch
First native mobile and tablet banking apps. The foundation every feature for the next decade built on. Phone, tablet, and wearable from day one.
Mobile + Tablet native apps shipped
Sep 2013
Apple ships Touch ID on the iPhone 5S. Biometrics go mainstream overnight. Most banks won't ship biometric login for another 2 years.
Identity & Fraud
Biometric Login for Mobile Banking
Touch ID and Android fingerprint. Among the first banks to ship.
60%+ biometric adoption
2012–14
Target, Home Depot, eBay. Massive data breaches hit the news monthly. Hundreds of millions of credentials exposed. Single-factor auth is a liability.
Identity & FraudTrust & Safety
Multi-Factor Auth at Enterprise Scale
Unified MFA across online and mobile, without tanking login completion rates.
25M+ users secured
2014–15
Account takeover attacks surge 100%+ YoY. Stolen credentials bypass MFA if attackers have the victim's phone. Most banks still rely on static rules.
Identity & FraudTrust & SafetyAI/ML
Device Fingerprinting & Behavioral Analytics
Continuous authentication using device signals and behavior patterns, not just at login.
-35% account takeover
2015–16
ATM skimming costs banks billions annually. Physical cards are the weakest link in the cash access chain. Nobody is questioning the card itself.
Identity & Fraud
Cardless ATM
Mobile-authenticated withdrawals across 12K+ ATMs. Eliminated the skimming vector entirely. Users chose it even when they had their card.
12K+ ATMs enabled
2016–2023

Fintech rewrites the rules of money.

Director, Product Management, Payments & Digital Channels · First Republic Bank
2017
Zelle launches, the banking industry's answer to Venmo. Every major bank scrambles to integrate before their customers move money outside the system entirely.
Payments
Zelle P2P for 1M+ High-Net-Worth Clients
Real-time P2P with fraud rules tailored to high-value transactions. Launched as the network went live.
+40% over forecast
2017–19
Betterment, Wealthfront, and Schwab Intelligent Portfolios rewrite wealth management. Automated investing goes mainstream. Every bank scrambles to add a robo offering.
AI/ML
Eagle Invest: Automated Advisory Platform
Launched 2016, before the mainstream wave. Brought algorithmic investing to a bank built on white-glove relationships.
$5K min vs. $250K+ traditional
First Republic · powered by BlackRock FutureAdvisor
2018–21
Enterprise cloud migration accelerates and core banking modernization becomes an industry obsession. Legacy systems can't support real-time, API-driven products.
Platform
Infrastructure Modernization: Cloud + Core Platform
Two migrations: data stack to cloud, then core platform off legacy. The foundation every client-facing product needed.
+18% YoY revenue post-migration
Mar 2020
COVID closes every bank branch in America. Millions of customers need to do everything digitally. Banks that aren't ready scramble. Some never recover.
Mobile
Running the Digital Channels
Loan closings, onboarding, wealth platform, data stack, all already digital. When branches closed, the infrastructure was already there.
Zero service disruption
2021
Intelligent document processing becomes a Gartner category. Banks are drowning in compliance paperwork: KYC, tax forms, legal filings. Most are still processing them manually or with brittle OCR.
AI/ML
DOC AI: Compliance Automation
Greenfield build. AI-powered extraction across 50+ document types with confidence scoring and human-in-the-loop review.
85% fully automated
May 2023: First Republic Bank is seized by the FDIC and acquired by JPMorgan Chase. Eight thousand employees learn their company no longer exists. Seven years of work, absorbed into the largest bank in the country. Within months, a VP seat opens at Wells Fargo. The skills were portable because they were never industry-specific. They were technology-specific.
